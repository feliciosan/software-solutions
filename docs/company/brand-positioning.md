## Why this document exists

Positioning decides who we are for and why they should trust us. Everything downstream (the website, the blog, the LinkedIn page, the topics we choose) is an expression of the positioning defined here. This is the source of truth. If another document contradicts it, this one wins until it is deliberately updated.

The test for every decision:

> When an engineer reads our content, they should think "these people really know this stack," not "another content mill."

---

## 1. Positioning statement

**Short form:**

> CodaCrew is an engineering publication and community for the modern JavaScript and TypeScript ecosystem.

**Expanded:**

> CodaCrew publishes practical, technically accurate content about building modern software: architecture, tooling, testing, CI/CD, performance, and the trends that matter. We share what we know so engineers ship better work, and we earn authority by being consistently useful, honest, and deep on the JS/TS ecosystem.

**Formal frame:**

- **For** software engineers working in (or moving into) the modern JavaScript and TypeScript stack,
- **who** want practical, trustworthy, no-fluff engineering content,
- **CodaCrew** is an engineering publication and community
- **that** teaches through real architecture, tooling, and testing knowledge across the JS/Node ecosystem,
- **unlike** generic tutorials, SEO content farms, and shallow AI-written posts,
- **because** we write from real experience building and scaling production systems, and we go deep instead of wide.

---

## 2. What we are, and what we are not

| We are | We are not |
| --- | --- |
| An engineering publication and community | A software agency or software house |
| A trusted source on the JS/TS ecosystem | A generalist blog chasing any keyword |
| Authors and engineers sharing knowledge | A vendor selling services |
| A LinkedIn-first technical brand | A personal-branding funnel |

We do not sell software services, and we do not discuss selling them. Readers are our **audience, community, subscribers, and fellow engineers**, never customers or leads.

---

## 3. The category we own

We compete for **attention and trust among engineers**, not for contracts. Our category is the small set of brands whose engineering content developers actually respect and return to (see [Competitors](./competitors.md)). We win by being deep, accurate, and consistent on one ecosystem rather than shallow across many.

## 4. Who we are for

Full detail in [Audience Profile](./ideal-customer-profile.md). In brief: senior and mid-level engineers, tech leads, staff engineers, engineering managers, and full-stack / JavaScript / TypeScript developers, plus engineers transitioning into modern stacks.

## 5. What we cover (authority area)

Our credibility is concentrated on the JavaScript / TypeScript ecosystem, end to end:

- **Frontend:** Next.js, React, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS.
- **Backend:** Node.js, NestJS, Express where appropriate.
- **Databases:** PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Firebase.
- **Infrastructure:** Docker, CI/CD, GitHub Actions, deployments, cloud platforms.
- **Quality:** Playwright, automated testing (unit, integration, end to end).
- **Architecture:** clean architecture, scalable systems, monorepos, API design, performance, observability, security, developer experience.

Full topic structure lives in [Topic Clusters](../marketing/topic-clusters.md). Every piece of content should reinforce CodaCrew as an authority in this ecosystem.

## 6. Positioning pillars (why engineers trust us)

1. **Depth over breadth.** We go deep on one ecosystem instead of shallow on everything.
2. **Real experience.** We write from building and scaling production systems, not from paraphrasing docs.
3. **Practical and honest.** Runnable examples, real trade-offs, and evidence-based opinions, including what does not work.
4. **Consistency.** Authority is compounding. We publish good work on a steady cadence.
5. **Craft.** Our own engineering standards are proof: if our site and code are excellent, our content is credible.

## 7. Messaging

**Primary message**
> Practical, deep engineering content for the modern JavaScript ecosystem.

**Supporting messages**
- Architecture, tooling, testing, and trends, explained by engineers who build.
- End-to-end knowledge: Next.js and NestJS, databases, CI/CD, and testing.
- No fluff, no filler, no shallow AI content.

---

## 8. Voice and language

Voice detail lives in [Brand Voice](./brand-voice.md). The essentials: write engineer-to-engineer, be specific and opinionated, show code, cite sources, and never pad. Avoid generic AI-sounding prose.

**House style:** never use the em dash character. Use commas, colons, or periods. Content ships in **en-US and pt-BR** with full parity.

**Vocabulary:** use audience, readers, community, engineers, subscribers, technical authority, educational content. Do not use client, customer, lead, sale, proposal, service delivery, agency, software house, or technical partner.
