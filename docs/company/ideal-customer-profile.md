## Why this document exists

Knowing exactly who we write for makes every editorial decision easier: which topics to prioritize, how deep to go, what tone to use, and where to publish. A sharp audience profile keeps us from writing generic content for no one.

---

## 1. The one-line audience

> Software engineers working in (or moving into) the modern JavaScript and TypeScript ecosystem, who want practical, deep, trustworthy content and enjoy learning from other engineers.

---

## 2. Audience personas

### Senior Engineers
- **Goals:** stay sharp, make better architecture and tooling decisions, learn from others' production experience.
- **Pain points:** shallow tutorials, outdated advice, no time for fluff.
- **Interests:** architecture, performance, testing strategy, trade-offs, internals.
- **Content preferences:** deep dives, opinionated analysis, real code.

### Mid-level Engineers
- **Goals:** level up toward senior, understand the "why" behind patterns.
- **Pain points:** knowing how but not when or why; gaps between tutorial and production.
- **Interests:** end-to-end examples, best practices, CI/CD, testing.
- **Content preferences:** practical guides with clear reasoning.

### Tech Leads
- **Goals:** raise their team's quality, pick the right tools and patterns, justify decisions.
- **Pain points:** balancing delivery with quality, evaluating new tech honestly.
- **Interests:** architecture decisions, standards, developer experience, adoption trade-offs.
- **Content preferences:** decision frameworks, comparisons, deep dives.

### Staff Engineers
- **Goals:** systemic impact, scalable architecture, org-wide standards.
- **Pain points:** scaling systems and practices, cutting through hype.
- **Interests:** distributed systems, observability, performance at scale, security.
- **Content preferences:** rigorous, evidence-based long-form.

### Engineering Managers
- **Goals:** keep current enough to lead well, share good material with their teams.
- **Pain points:** less hands-on time, need signal over noise.
- **Interests:** trends, tooling, quality practices, team-level engineering topics.
- **Content preferences:** clear summaries, trend analysis, shareable pieces.

### JavaScript and TypeScript Developers
- **Goals:** master the language and its ecosystem.
- **Pain points:** fast-moving ecosystem, fragmented information.
- **Interests:** TypeScript patterns, Node.js, framework specifics (Next.js, NestJS).
- **Content preferences:** focused, example-rich articles.

### Full-stack Developers
- **Goals:** be effective across the stack without going shallow.
- **Pain points:** breadth vs depth, connecting frontend and backend well.
- **Interests:** Next.js + NestJS, databases (PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Firebase), APIs.
- **Content preferences:** end-to-end builds and integrations.

### Engineers transitioning into modern stacks
- **Goals:** move from an older stack into modern JS/TS confidently.
- **Pain points:** knowing what is current, avoiding outdated tutorials.
- **Interests:** foundations, migration paths, "the modern way" to do X.
- **Content preferences:** clear, current, well-scoped guides.

---

## 3. What the audience values (across personas)

- **Depth and accuracy** over volume and hype.
- **Real code and real trade-offs**, not toy examples.
- **Current** information for a fast-moving ecosystem.
- **Honesty**, including what does not work.
- **Respect for their time**: no fluff, no padding.

---

## 4. Where they are

- **LinkedIn** (our primary growth channel and where much of the professional engineering audience engages). See [LinkedIn Growth Playbook](../marketing/linkedin-growth-playbook.md).
- **Search** (engineers searching real questions land on the blog).
- **Developer communities** (Reddit, Hacker News, Discords, dev.to) where good content gets shared.

---

## 5. Who we are NOT writing for

- Absolute beginners looking for "learn to code" basics (we assume working engineers).
- People wanting non-technical business content.
- Readers looking for shallow, listicle-style filler.

Naming the anti-audience keeps our depth intact.

---

## 6. Maintaining this document

Review quarterly and whenever analytics or comments reveal who is actually reading and engaging. Let real audience behavior, not assumptions, sharpen these personas over time.
