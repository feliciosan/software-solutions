## Why this document exists

The ICP is the filter that protects everything else. A sharp ICP makes marketing cheaper (we know exactly who we are talking to), sales faster (we qualify hard and early), and delivery better (we work on problems we are excellent at). A blurry ICP does the opposite: generic copy, long sales cycles, and projects that drain the team.

This document defines **who we are for, who we are not for, and how to tell the difference quickly.** It is opinionated by design. Turning away good-but-wrong-fit work is a feature.

---

## 1. The one-sentence ICP

> Early-stage founders and small product teams who need to turn an idea into a scalable digital product, value senior engineering and partnership over the lowest price, and want to move fast without creating technical debt they will regret.

Everything below expands and operationalizes this sentence.

---

## 2. Primary segments

We serve three closely related segments. They share the same core need (turn an idea into a scalable product) at different stages.

### Segment A: The idea-stage founder
- Has conviction and often some early validation, but no product yet.
- Needs an MVP built and shipped to real users quickly.
- Fears wrong technical decisions and slow progress most of all.
- Best fit for: [MVP Development](../sales/service-catalog.md).

### Segment B: The scaling startup
- Has a live product with traction that is starting to strain.
- Needs to scale a SaaS, add capabilities, or harden architecture.
- Fears downtime, rewrites, and losing velocity as complexity grows.
- Best fit for: SaaS Development, AI Integrations.

### Segment C: The team that needs senior reinforcement
- Has an in-house team but a gap in senior capacity or a specific expertise.
- Needs experienced engineers who integrate fast and raise the bar.
- Fears onboarding drag and mismatched seniority.
- Best fit for: Team Augmentation, Product Modernization.

## 4. The psychographics that actually predict fit

Firmographics get you close; mindset is what predicts a great engagement. Strong-fit customers tend to:

- **Treat engineering as core, not a cost to minimize.** They want it done right.
- **Want a partner, not a pair of hands.** They value opinion and push-back.
- **Move fast and decide fast.** They can answer product questions without a month of committees.
- **Care about the product they will own,** not just the deliverable.
- **Understand that quality now is speed later.**

## 5. Jobs to be done (what they are really hiring us for)

Founders "hire" CodaCrew to do one or more of these jobs:

1. "Get my idea in front of real users fast, without betting the company on the wrong architecture."
2. "Give me senior engineering I could not otherwise assemble or afford to hire."
3. "Be the technical co-founder I do not have, and tell me the truth about trade-offs."
4. "Help my product scale without a rewrite or an outage."
5. "Reinforce my team with people who make it better, not slower."

Marketing and sales messages should map to these jobs, not to our feature list. See [Content Strategy](../marketing/content-strategy.md).

## 6. Anti-ICP: who we are NOT for

Naming the anti-ICP is as important as naming the ICP. We are a poor fit for, and should decline or redirect:

- **Buyers who want the cheapest option.** Price shoppers recategorize us as a commodity. See [Brand Positioning](./brand-positioning.md).
- **Simple institutional websites or one-page landing pages.** Good work, not our work.
- **Clients who refuse involvement in technical decisions.** Radical clarity (see [Values](./values.md)) requires a partner who participates.
- **"Just execute my exact spec, no questions."** If they do not want product thinking, they do not want CodaCrew.
- **Enterprise procurement with committee buying and RFP theater.** Long cycles, low trust, wrong game.
- **Anyone expecting guaranteed outcomes with no discovery,** or a "miraculous quick fix."

This maps directly to the "Doesn't make sense if" list on the website's Audience section. Keep them in sync.

## 7. Qualification: how to tell fast

Use these signals in the first conversation. They are drawn from real friction points.

**Green flags**
- Founder has direct decision authority and can move quickly.
- Talks about users, traction, and the business, not just "a website."
- Wants to understand trade-offs and asks good questions.
- Sees this as the start of a relationship, not a transaction.

**Yellow flags (proceed with a clear frame)**
- Fixed, immovable budget below the value of the work: reframe scope, do not discount quality.
- Vague product vision: sell a discovery/architecture engagement first.

**Red flags (usually decline or redirect)**
- "What is your hourly rate?" as the first and main question.
- Wants the cheapest bid and is comparing on price alone.
- Refuses to be involved in decisions.
- Expects guarantees with no discovery.
- Committee buying with no direct access to the decision maker.

Qualification questions and scripts live in [Proposal Guidelines](../sales/proposal-guidelines.md) and [Client Onboarding](../sales/client-onboarding.md).

## 8. How the ICP shapes each function

- **Marketing:** every page and article speaks to founders in the segments above, using their language and jobs-to-be-done. Off-ICP topics are out of scope.
- **Sales:** qualify against Section 7 early. A fast, kind "no" protects the pipeline and the team.
- **Delivery:** the best engagements are with in-profile clients; over-indexing on off-profile work is a leading indicator of team strain.
- **Content backlog:** article ideas are prioritized by how directly they serve an ICP job to be done. See [Content Backlog](../marketing/content-backlog.md).

---

## 9. Maintaining this document

- **Review cadence:** quarterly, and after any run of engagements that felt wrong. Off-profile projects that slipped through are the best raw material for sharpening this document.
- **Evolve deliberately:** widening the ICP to fill the pipeline is the most common and most expensive mistake a services company makes. Widen only with intent, never out of scarcity.
- **Keep it in sync** with the website Audience section and [Brand Positioning](./brand-positioning.md).
