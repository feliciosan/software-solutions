## Why this document exists

The mission is the fixed point we navigate by. Strategy, topics, and channels change; the mission does not. When a decision is genuinely hard, we resolve it by asking which option serves the mission.

---

## 1. Mission statement

> **We help software engineers continuously improve by publishing practical, modern, technically accurate engineering content.**

Every word matters:

- **Software engineers.** Our audience is people who build software, from mid-level to staff (see [Audience Profile](./ideal-customer-profile.md)).
- **Continuously improve.** We exist to make readers better at their craft, one article at a time.
- **Publishing.** We are a publication. Consistent, high-quality output is the job.
- **Practical, modern, technically accurate.** Real, current, correct. If it is not useful and true, it does not ship.
- **Engineering content.** Centered on the JavaScript / TypeScript ecosystem we know deeply.

---

## 2. Vision

> A place engineers trust as one of the best sources for learning how to build modern software in the JavaScript ecosystem.

Good engineering knowledge is scattered, shallow, or drowned in SEO filler and AI-generated noise. We exist to be the opposite: deep, honest, and genuinely useful, so that when an engineer has a real question about Next.js, NestJS, testing, or architecture, our name is one they reach for.

---

## 3. How we pursue it

1. **Publish excellent content** on the website blog, consistently (see [Content Strategy](../marketing/content-strategy.md)).
2. **Grow the community** through the LinkedIn company page (see [LinkedIn Growth Playbook](../marketing/linkedin-growth-playbook.md)).
3. **Go deep on one ecosystem** rather than shallow on everything.
4. **Prove our expertise through our own craft**, so the content is credible.

---

## 4. How we measure it

We prefer signals of genuine usefulness over vanity numbers:

- **Reach and trust:** returning readers, engaged followers, articles engineers save and share.
- **Authority:** ranking for real engineering questions, being cited or referenced by other engineers.
- **Depth:** content people finish and act on, not bounce off.
- **Consistency:** a steady cadence of work we are proud of.

An article that gets clicks but teaches nothing is a mission failure, even if the numbers look good.

---

## 5. What the mission is not

- It is **not** "publish as much as possible." Quality and accuracy beat volume.
- It is **not** "chase whatever ranks." We write what genuinely helps engineers in our ecosystem.
- It is **not** a funnel to sell anything. We do not sell services, and we do not discuss selling them.

---

## 6. Maintaining this document

The mission should change rarely. Revisit only if our fundamental reason for existing changes, not when tactics or topics change. Review annually alongside [Values](./values.md).
