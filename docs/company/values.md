## Why this document exists

Values are only real if they cost something. This document lists the principles CodaCrew is willing to publish slower, or not at all, to uphold. Each includes what it looks like in practice and the trade-off it forces. Use it when a decision feels wrong but you cannot say why.

Each value has four parts: **the principle**, **in practice**, **the trade-off**, and **violation looks like**.

---

## 1. Technical accuracy

**Principle.** If it is not correct, it does not ship.
**In practice.** We test the code we publish, verify claims against primary sources, and version-pin examples. We correct mistakes fast and openly.
**The trade-off.** Accuracy is slower than paraphrasing docs. We publish less, but readers can trust it.
**Violation looks like.** Untested snippets, hand-wavy claims, copying an assertion without checking it.

---

## 2. Practicality

**Principle.** Content should help an engineer do something better today.
**In practice.** Real examples, real trade-offs, runnable code. We answer the question the reader actually has.
**The trade-off.** Practical writing takes real work to produce; theory is cheaper.
**Violation looks like.** Abstract posts with no code, "it depends" with no follow-through.

---

## 3. Curiosity

**Principle.** We stay genuinely interested in how things work and why.
**In practice.** We dig into internals, try new tools honestly, and follow the ecosystem closely.
**The trade-off.** Curiosity costs time that does not always become an article.
**Violation looks like.** Recycling the same safe topics, ignoring what is actually changing in the stack.

---

## 4. Continuous learning

**Principle.** We are engineers who keep getting better, in public.
**In practice.** We share lessons learned, including from our own mistakes, and update our views with new evidence.
**The trade-off.** Learning in public means admitting we did not always know.
**Violation looks like.** Pretending to be finished, defending an outdated take out of ego.

---

## 5. Engineering craftsmanship

**Principle.** Our own work is the proof of our content.
**In practice.** The site, the code, and the examples are held to the [Engineering Standards](../engineering/engineering-standards.md). Quality is visible.
**The trade-off.** Craft is invisible until it saves you, and it is never the fastest path.
**Violation looks like.** A sloppy site or careless examples while claiming engineering authority.

---

## 6. Open knowledge

**Principle.** We give the good stuff away.
**In practice.** No artificial gates, no bait-and-switch, no withholding the useful part.
**The trade-off.** Generosity does not convert directly to anything; it compounds slowly into trust.
**Violation looks like.** Teasing value without delivering it, thin posts that exist only to rank.

---

## 7. Community contribution

**Principle.** We add to the ecosystem, not just extract attention from it.
**In practice.** We credit sources, engage honestly in discussion, and help fellow engineers in comments and replies.
**The trade-off.** Real engagement takes time that does not scale.
**Violation looks like.** Broadcasting only, engagement bait, taking ideas without attribution.

---

## 8. Simplicity

**Principle.** Clear beats clever, in code and in writing.
**In practice.** Plain language, minimal jargon (defined when used), the simplest example that proves the point.
**The trade-off.** Simple is harder to write than complicated.
**Violation looks like.** Showing off, complexity for its own sake, walls of undefined jargon.

---

## 9. Evidence-based opinions

**Principle.** We take positions, and we back them.
**In practice.** Opinions are grounded in benchmarks, experience, or sources. We say when something is preference versus fact.
**The trade-off.** Backing a claim is more work than asserting it.
**Violation looks like.** Hot takes with nothing behind them, hype, or fear as a substitute for evidence.

---

## Using these values

- **What to publish:** when unsure, pick the option that upholds more values, even if it is slower.
- **Editorial review:** these values are the backbone of the [Editorial Guidelines](../marketing/editorial-guidelines.md) checklist.
- **Bilingual and inclusive:** we publish en-US and pt-BR with equal quality, and we write for a global engineering audience.

---

## Maintaining this document

Values change slowly. Add one only when we have repeatedly made the same hard call. Remove one only if we honestly no longer live it. Review annually alongside [Mission](./mission.md).
