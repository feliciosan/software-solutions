## Why this document exists

This is the living, prioritized list of what we will write next. [Content Strategy](./content-strategy.md) sets the principles; this is where those principles become a queue. It exists so we never stare at a blank page wondering what to publish, and so every article is chosen on purpose rather than on whim.

Keep it current. When a post ships, mark it Published with its slug. When an idea proves weak, drop it. Add ideas as they come from sales calls, search data, and reader questions.

---

## 1. How we prioritize

Each idea is scored on three axes (High / Medium / Low), from [Content Strategy](./content-strategy.md) section 6:

- **ICP fit:** does it answer a real question an in-profile founder has?
- **Demand:** search volume or how often it comes up in sales conversations.
- **Right to speak:** do we have genuine senior experience to make it distinctly ours?

**Priority = impact over effort.** A High/High/High idea that is quick to write jumps the queue. Anything that is not clearly founder-facing gets reframed toward founder consequences or dropped (we do not write engineer-to-engineer tutorials, per [Content Strategy](./content-strategy.md)).

## 7. Article production checklist

When a backlog item is picked up, it moves through: Idea, Drafting, In review, Localizing, Ready, Published. It is not Published until it clears [Blog Standards](./blog-standards.md) and the [SEO Checklist](../website/seo-checklist.md), and exists in both locales.

---

## 8. Backlog review cadence

- Review every two to four weeks: reprioritize, add new intake, retire dead ideas.
- After each publish, update the Published table and pull the next high-priority item.
- Let search data and sales conversations, not personal preference, drive what rises to the top.

## Maintaining this document

This is the most frequently updated marketing document. Keep it honest and current: an out-of-date backlog is worse than none, because it hides what actually needs writing. Titles here are working titles; the final title is set per [Blog Standards](./blog-standards.md) at publish time.
