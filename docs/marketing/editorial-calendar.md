## Why this document exists

Authority compounds from consistency. This document is how we turn the [Content Backlog](./content-backlog.md) into a realistic, repeatable publishing rhythm, so we ship good work steadily instead of in bursts. It plans articles and their LinkedIn distribution together.

Principle: **a cadence we can actually hold, at a quality we are proud of.** Better one excellent article a week than four rushed ones.

---

## 1. Cadence (starting point)

Pick a sustainable baseline and protect it. A sane starting rhythm for a two-person team:

- **1 deep article per week** on the blog (adjust down before you adjust quality).
- **2 to 4 LinkedIn posts per week** on the company page (article repurposes plus standalone insights). See [LinkedIn Growth Playbook](./linkedin-growth-playbook.md).
- **1 trend/analysis piece per month** reacting to what changed in the ecosystem.

If a week is tight, ship a smaller, still-excellent piece rather than skipping or lowering the bar.

---

## 2. Balance across clusters

Rotate across [Topic Clusters](./topic-clusters.md) so no single cluster dominates and the whole ecosystem is covered over time. A rough monthly mix:

- 1 frontend (Next.js/React/TypeScript)
- 1 backend (NestJS/Node)
- 1 quality or infra (Testing/CI/CD)
- 1 architecture, databases, performance, DX, AI, or trends (rotating)

Deepen a cluster deliberately when we want to build a pillar page.

---

## 3. Planning horizon

- **Now (this week):** the article in production and its LinkedIn posts.
- **Next (this month):** 3 to 4 titles selected from the high-priority backlog, assigned a rough week and cluster.
- **Later (this quarter):** themes and pillar goals (for example, "build out the Testing cluster").

Keep the plan lightweight (a simple table or board). The backlog holds ideas; the calendar holds commitments.

---

## 4. Article lifecycle in the calendar

Each planned item moves: Planned -> Drafting -> In review -> Localizing -> Ready -> Published, then Distributed. An item is only "Published" when it clears [Blog Standards](./blog-standards.md) in both locales.

---

## 5. Repurposing plan

Every article is planned with its distribution, not as an afterthought:

- Day of publish: company-page insight post (link in first comment).
- Within the week: optionally a carousel or code-snippet post derived from the same article.
- Ongoing: internal links from new articles back to it.

---

## 7. Review cadence

Plan the next month at month-end: pull high-priority backlog items, assign weeks and clusters, and check the cluster balance. Adjust the baseline cadence only deliberately, never by silently dropping quality.

---

## Maintaining this document

Keep the horizon current and honest. If we consistently miss the cadence, lower the baseline rather than lower the bar. Move shipped items into a short "published this quarter" log for momentum.
