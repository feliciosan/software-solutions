## Why this document exists

LinkedIn is our primary channel for growing an engineering audience. This playbook covers how we grow the **CodaCrew company page**: cadence, formats, engagement, and metrics. The per-article "publish then distribute" routine lives in [Social Distribution](./social-distribution.md); this is the deeper growth strategy.

> The company page is the primary public voice. Founders' personal profiles are intentionally not the primary channel due to employment constraints.

---

## 1. Goal

Grow a genuine following of engineers who trust CodaCrew as a source of quality technical content, and drive them to read the blog. We optimize for **relevant reach and engagement**, not raw follower vanity.

---

## 2. Posting cadence

- **2 to 4 posts per week**, consistent. Consistency beats bursts.
- At least one post per week reinforces a blog article; the rest can be standalone insights, snippets, or discussion posts.
- Protect the cadence; if a week is tight, post a smaller value-first insight rather than nothing.

---

## 3. Content formats

Rotate formats to serve different behaviors (reading, saving, discussing):

- **Insight post:** one sharp, specific technical point, well explained. The workhorse.
- **Carousel:** a step-by-step or "N things about X" in slides. Excellent for saves and dwell time. Great for turning an article into a scannable sequence.
- **Code snippet:** a small, clean snippet (screenshot or formatted) plus the "why it matters". Engineers love concrete code.
- **Discussion post:** an honest opinion or a real trade-off question that invites debate ("When would you actually reach for tRPC over REST?").
- **Article repurpose:** the core insight of a blog post, link in the first comment.

Keep visuals on-brand (Moss & Stone, clean, technical). Avoid stock-photo clichés.

---

## 4. Post structure (what performs)

- **Hook (first 1 to 2 lines):** a concrete, specific claim or question. This is what shows before "see more". No throat-clearing.
- **Body:** short lines, whitespace, one idea, real value delivered in the post itself.
- **Takeaway:** the one line to remember.
- **Link in the first comment** (protects reach), with a "full write-up in comments" nudge.
- **A genuine question** at the end to invite replies.
- 3 to 5 relevant hashtags max (for example #javascript #typescript #nextjs #nodejs #webdev).

---

## 5. Engagement strategy

Reach on LinkedIn is earned by conversation, not just posting.

- Reply to every meaningful comment within the first hours (this drives the algorithm and relationships).
- Spend 10 to 15 minutes per posting day commenting substantively on other engineers' and brands' posts.
- Ask real questions; treat replies as the start of relationships and future article ideas.
- Engage from the page where possible; keep it professional and genuinely helpful.

---

## 6. Growth tactics

- **Repurpose every article** into at least one post; strong articles into a carousel plus an insight post spaced apart.
- **Series:** themed weeks around a cluster (for example a "Testing week") build momentum and follows.
- **Invite discussion** on opinions and trade-offs; debate posts travel.
- **Cross-pollinate:** link blog articles from posts and reference posts in the blog where relevant.
- **Consistency over virality:** a steady stream of useful posts compounds; do not chase one viral hit.

---

## 7. Metrics

Track weekly:

- **Follower growth** (and whether followers are engineers, our target audience).
- **Impressions**, and more importantly **saves, comments, and shares** (value signals).
- **Click-through** to the blog (PostHog referral).
- **Best-performing hooks and formats**, fed back into the [Content Backlog](./content-backlog.md) and [Editorial Calendar](./editorial-calendar.md).

Review monthly: double down on what works, drop what does not.

---

## 8. Anti-patterns

- Broadcasting links with no standalone value.
- Posting and never engaging.
- Engagement bait, hype, or clickbait hooks.
- Inconsistent posting (going quiet for weeks).
- Any selling or "customer" framing.

---

## Maintaining this document

Review monthly against real growth and engagement data. Promote tactics that consistently work; retire the rest. Keep it practical and specific.
